## The EE Challenge: Dispatch & Deliver

**Welcome!** This challenge is your opportunity to show us not just what you can deliver, but also how you go about it.

We know that real-world problems are rarely perfectly specified, so we’ve kept this brief **deliberately open-ended**. We aren't looking for a "finished" product; we are looking for you to have **chosen and laid out** your development path so that you can implement a production ready part of the solution that **best demonstrates your talents.**

**Timebox:** Please spend around 2 hours on this. We respect your time and do not expect a completed system. We would rather see a solid, working fragment with a clear direction of travel.

**This test is deliberately open-ended.** We would like you to use your judgement to pick a slice of the problem which shows your skills in the time allotted.

### The Scenario

Our client, "Rhine," needs a system to help warehouse operatives dispatch parcels with speed and accuracy. Operatives need to be assigned orders, told what to pack, and directed to the correct inventory locations.

### The Task

Design and build a responsive, accessible Web UI for our order packing system. Your solution must prioritize business objectives and consider likely problems.
We are happy for you to mock the Service Layer, but we would like you to demonstrate how your UI expects to interact with it.

### Business Objectives

- **Efficiency is Key:** Operatives need to work efficiently. Every second counts.
- **Reliability:** We want every parcel shipped once and soon.
- **Quality:** The solution must work from both a functional and non-functional perspective. We don’t want the first notification of a problem to be a customer complaint.

### The Focus

We aren't looking for a "finished" product; we want to see a production-quality fragment of the solution that demonstrates your talents. Show us how you model any domain and service boundaries and justify the architectural choices you made within your chosen scope.

### How We Work

**This test is deliberately open-ended**. We would like you to use your judgement to pick a slice of the problem which shows your skills in the time allotted.

1. **Use Your Tools (AI Included):** We’re happy for you to use any tool including LLMs, but we do need to see a clear signal that you have the skill level to join our network as a senior developer. We expect you to curate and guide any LLM involvement and to be sure that the solution you submit demonstrates your talents, not just the tool’s. Please briefly document your "partnership"—did it write boilerplate, brainstorm edge cases, or refactor a function?
2. **Simplicity & Maintainability** EE values simplicity. We value clean, readable code. If a new developer joined the team today, could they understand your logic immediately?
3. **Quality** A solution without tests is a liability. Please include testing at appropriate levels for the specific slice you choose to implement.
4. **Show Your Working** Since you only have 2 hours, you will make assumptions. Please summarise them in your response.
   - What did you decide not to build and why?
   - Why did you choose this specific tech stack?
   - If you had more time, what would you do next?

## Frequently asked questions

### Q: I’m not clear on what I’m to implement here

We’ve laid out that the user is an operative in a warehouse looking to see which package to assemble next, what to put in it, and where to go to find the contents. We’re not expecting you to deliver a complete solution, rather to develop a part of the solution which demonstrates your abilities and the way you would approach a task like this. There’s no right or wrong choice here; you may pick to define and implement a single service, or multiple services and show us how they fit together.

### Q: How much of the code should be "mine" vs. the LLM’s?

We care about the final output and your ability to justify it. It’s fine to use an LLM to generate code, but make sure you’re happy with it, is the solution something you’re prepared to submit as your own work? Are you happy that it meets reasonable requirements for that piece of code? Does the solution still demonstrate your tallents as a developer?

### Q: Can I make up my own business rules?

Yes! Real-world requirements are often "noisy." If you decide that an operative must scan a "Bin Location" before they can scan an "Item," implement that and tell us why you made that choice. Your ability to model the domain is what we are assessing.

### Is there a specific tech stack I should use?

We’re platform agnostic, but the majority of our clients use React. Use the tools that allow you to be most effective, we want to see how you structure your code to be maintainable. Part of the task is justifying your choice—tell us why your choices are the right ones for this problem.
